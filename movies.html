Studio Ghibli Movies

A list of Ghibli most popular movies - with further information and ratings!

Spirited Away

Released in July 20, 2001

About The film is about the adventures of a young ten-year-old girl named Chihiro as she wanders into the world of the gods and spirits. She is forced to work at a bathhouse following her parents being turned into pigs by the witch Yubaba.
Director and Screenwriter(s) Hayao Miyazaki
Producer(s) Toshio Suzuki
Music (Composer) Joe Hisaishi
Rating 97%
Howl's Moving Castle

Released in November 20, 2004

About The film is based on the 1986 novel of the same name (translated in Japan as The Wizard's Howl and the Devil of Fire) by British writer Diana Wynne Jones and is about a young milliner named Sophie, who is transformed into an old woman by a witch, and the wizard Howl.
Director and Screenwriter(s) Hayao Miyazaki
Producer(s) Toshio Suzuki
Music (Composer) Joe Hisaishi
Rating 87%
My Neighbour Totoro

Released in April 16, 1988

About The film, which is set Tokorozawa City, Saitama Prefecture, tells the story of a professor's two young daughters (Satsuki and Mei) and their interactions with friendly wood spirits in post-war rural Japan. It stars the voice actors Noriko Hidaka, Chika Sakamoto, and Hitoshi Takagi.
Director and Screenwriter(s) Hayao Miyazaki
Producer(s) Tōru Hara
Music (Composer) Joe Hisaishi
Rating 85%
Kiki's Delivery Service

Released in July 29, 1989

About In this anime feature, 13-year-old Kiki moves to a seaside town with her talking cat, Jiji, to spend a year alone, in accordance with her village's tradition for witches in training. After learning to control her broomstick, Kiki sets up a flying courier service and soon becomes a fixture in the community. But when the insecure young witch begins questioning herself and loses her magic abilities, she must overcome her self-doubt to get her powers back.
Director and Screenwriter(s) Hayao Miyazaki
Producer(s) Hayao Miyazaki
Music (Composer) Joe Hisaishi
Rating 98%
When Marnie Was There

Released in July 19, 2014

About It is based on Joan G. Robinson's same-named novel. It transposes the setting from Norfolk, England in the original novel to Hokkaido, Japan. The film follows Anna Sasaki living with her relatives in the seaside town. Anna comes across a nearby abandoned mansion, where she meets Marnie, a mysterious girl who asks her to promise to keep her secrets from everyone.
Director and Screenwriter(s) Hiromasa Yonebayashi, Hiromasa Yonebayashi, Keiko Niwa, Masashi Ando
Producer(s) Yoshiaki Nishimura
Music (Composer) Takatsugu Muramatsu
Rating 91%
Grave of the Fireflies

Released in April 16, 1988

About This film is set in the city of Kobe and Nishinomiya, Hyōgo Prefecture, the film tells the story of two siblings, Seita and Setsuko Yokokawa, and their desperate struggle to survive during the final months of the Second World War. This is the first film produced by Shinchosha Publishing, who hired Studio Ghibli to do the animation production work
Director and Screenwriter(s) Isao Takahata
Producer(s) Tōru Hara
Music (Composer) Michio Mamiya
Rating 100%
Ponyo

Released in July 19, 2008

About During a forbidden excursion to see the surface world, a goldfish princess encounters a human boy named Sosuke, who gives her the name Ponyo. Ponyo longs to become human, and as her friendship with Sosuke grows, she becomes more humanlike. Ponyo's father brings her back to their ocean kingdom, but so strong is Ponyo's wish to live on the surface that she breaks free, and in the process, spills a collection of magical elixirs that endanger Sosuke's village.
Director and Screenwriter(s) Hayao Miyazaki
Producer(s) Toshio Suzuki
Music (Composer) Joe Hisaishi
Rating 91%
Princess Mononoke

Released in July 12, 1997

About In the 14th century, the harmony that humans, animals and gods have enjoyed begins to crumble. The protagonist, young Ashitaka - infected by an animal attack, seeks a cure from the deer-like god Shishigami. In his travels, he sees humans ravaging the earth, bringing down the wrath of wolf god Moro and his human companion Princess Mononoke. Hiskattempts to broker peace between her and the humans brings only conflict.
Director and Screenwriter(s) Hayao Miyazaki
Producer(s) Toshio Suzuki
Music (Composer) Joe Hisaishi
Rating 93%
Castle in the Sky

Released in August 2, 1986

About Young orphan Sheeta and her kidnapper, Col. Muska, are flying to a military prison when their plane is attacked by a gang of air pirates led by the matronly Dola. Escaping from a mid-air collision via a magic crystal around her neck, Sheeta meets fellow orphan Pazu and the pair join forces to discover the mystical floating city of Laputa while pursued by both Muska and the pirates, who lust for the city's myriad treasures.
Director and Screenwriter(s) Hayao Miyazaki
Producer(s) Isao Takahata
Music (Composer) Joe Hisaishi
Rating 96%
Whisper of the Heart

Released in July 15, 1995

About Based on the manga with the same title, this animated film follows Shizuku, an inquisitive young girl and a voracious reader, who longs to be a writer when she grows up. One day she notices that all of her library books have previously been taken out by one Seiji Amasawa. Amid chasing after a large cat, befriending an eccentric antiques dealer and writing her first novel, Shizuku aims to find this mysterious boy who may well be her soul mate.
Director and Screenwriter(s) Yoshifumi Kondō & Hayao Miyazaki
Producer(s) Toshio Suzuki
Music (Composer) Yuji Nomi
Rating 94%
The Wind Rises

Released in July 20, 2013

About This film is about the lifelong love of flight inspires Japanese aviation engineer Jiro Horikoshi (Hideaki Anno), whose storied career includes the creation of the A6M World War II fighter plane.
Director and Screenwriter(s) Hayao Miyazaki
Producer(s) Toshio Suzuki
Music (Composer) Joe Hisaishi
Rating 88%
The Cat Returns

Released in July 19, 2002

About High school student Haru (Chizuru Ikewaki) rescues a cat that was about to be run over by a truck and discovers the cat is actually a prince named Lune. Out of gratitude, Lune's father, the Cat King, asks her to marry Lune. Haru is brought to the Cat Kingdom, where she starts to develop feline features. When she is prevented from leaving, the Baron (Yoshihiko Hakamada) and Toto, two statues that have magically been given life, provide assistance in gaining her freedom.
Director and Screenwriter(s) Hiroyuki Morita, Reiko Yoshida
Producer(s) Toshio Suzuki, Nozomu Takahashi
Music (Composer) Yuji Nomi
Rating 91%
From Up on Poppy Hill

Released in July 16, 2011

About It's 1963 and Japan is in the midst of swift modernisation, leaving behind the Second World War's painful memories and focusing on a brighter future, symbolised by the coming year's Tokyo Olympics.
Director and Screenwriter(s) Gorō Miyazaki
Producer(s) Hayao Miyazaki, Keiko Niwa
Music (Composer) Satoshi Takebe
Rating 86%
Arrietty

Released in July 17, 2010

About Arrietty, a tiny teenager, lives with her parents in the recesses of a suburban home, unbeknown to the homeowner and housekeeper. Like others of her kind, Arrietty remains hidden from her human hosts, but occasionally ventures forth from beneath the floorboards to borrow sugar cubes and other supplies. A secret friendship forms when 12-year-old Shawn meets Arrietty, but their relationship could spell danger for Arrietty's family.
Director and Screenwriter(s) Hiromasa Yonebayashi, Hayao Miyazaki, Keiko Niwa
Producer(s) Toshio Suzuki
Music (Composer) Cécile Corbel
Rating 95%
The Tale of the Princess Kaguya

Released in November 23, 2013

About A tiny nymph found inside a bamboo stalk grows into a beautiful and desirable young woman, who orders her suitors to prove their love by completing a series of near-impossible tasks.
Director and Screenwriter(s) Isao Takahata, Isao Takahata, Riko Sakaguchi
Producer(s) Yoshiaki Nishimura
Music (Composer) Joe Hisaishi
Rating 100%
